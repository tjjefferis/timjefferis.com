This little post
draws upon an <a href="https://www.leadershipmatters.org.uk/leadership-personality-tool/">online
diagnostic tool </a>that I've used as an aid to self-reflection. The tool purports to describe your personality and so underline strengths and weaknesses. It does this by presenting a series of binary choices - both with merits - and forcing you to make a decision.<o:p></o:p>

My personality type
according to the Leadership Matters tool is: ISTJ. Where this equates to:<o:p></o:p>

<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjf4AVq_pKrW6zO_aM7LSGXUk_3KF0jMLbw0aOusqYTkQXB9pnkaWCEpFQuOr3h09C-FOdxtf63Eixq7iBzOXO4bn2LRL2RTt6_g0wDLMyMHEEenuujSArMY7W4HfIBO4MaKafabQE31GQ/" imageanchor="1"><img border="0" data-original-height="637" data-original-width="968" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjf4AVq_pKrW6zO_aM7LSGXUk_3KF0jMLbw0aOusqYTkQXB9pnkaWCEpFQuOr3h09C-FOdxtf63Eixq7iBzOXO4bn2LRL2RTt6_g0wDLMyMHEEenuujSArMY7W4HfIBO4MaKafabQE31GQ/"/></a>

In summary (a bit <a href="https://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=Barnum%20Statement">Barnum-statementy</a>
perhaps?) the tool says about me:

<ul><li>You are naturally
thoughtful, diligent and like things to be done properly</li><li>You will often take
charge of situations to make sure things are done efficiently</li><li>You might benefit
from being more open to other people’s ideas or ways of doing things</li></ul>

Then it continues:<o:p></o:p>

<b>YOUR NATURAL STRENGTHS ARE…</b><b><o:p></o:p></b>

<ul><li>You are very
reliable and committed</li><li>You are well
organised, polite and courteous</li><li>You make sure
things are done to the proper standard</li><li>You ensure sure the
detail is right and plan things thoroughly in advance</li><li>You are not afraid
to tackle poor performance</li><li>You are happy to
assume control of situations and get things organised</li><li>You communicate
clearly and meticulously</li><li>You are someone who
can hold a team to account so it does the right thing, not the easy thing</li></ul>

<b>YOU ARE LIKELY TO BE MOST PRODUCTIVE IF…</b><b><o:p></o:p></b>

<ul><li>You are working
with other people who work hard and put in the effort</li><li>Things are done
properly first time and don’t need correcting</li><li>People argue with
you or take things you say too personally</li><li>You are operating
within a clear stable structure, where roles and systems are clearly defined</li><li>Your boss respects
what you bring to the team</li><li>You are given clear
direction and a degree of autonomy</li><li>People respect your
privacy</li><li>Change happens
gradually and is based on what has worked somewhere else</li></ul><b>YOU MAY BE LESS PRODUCTIVE IF…</b><br/><ul><li>People don’t
properly look at all the evidence when making a decision</li><li>People are
inefficient or sloppy</li><li>People are moaning
or whinging</li><li>People with less
experience than you think they know it all</li><li>Colleagues rely on
their intuition or ‘gut-feelings’ rather than the facts of the matter</li><li>People are
over-familiar</li><li>Decisions are made
in a rush</li><li>Your boss
micro-manages you</li></ul>

<b>YOU MAY BECOME MORE EFFECTIVE BY…</b><b><o:p></o:p></b>

<ul><li>Going out of your
way to consult with others more when deciding on something</li><li>Not giving the
impression (often without realising) that you are always right</li><li>Taking more time to
stand back and see the big picture</li><li>Remembering that
some decisions are best left open for a while</li><li>Being a bit more
open about your own feelings – showing your human side more</li><li>Not being afraid to
let your hair down sometimes</li><li>Asking questions of
others in a way that avoids them feeling interrogated by you</li><li>Asking others for
their ideas rather than dismissing those who challenge the status quo</li></ul>

So there you have it. On balance not a bad pen-picture for a 1-minute test!
