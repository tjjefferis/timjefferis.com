It's very easy to start feeling lost with the scale and complexity of the job of being a school leader. Especially at times like these, when fires are burning all over the place, it can be hard to step back and see where everything is going.<br/>

<div>
I recently watched <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g5c6a9kw-3E&amp;t=3372s">this presentation</a> by <a href="https://twitter.com/Barker_J">@Barker_J</a> and <a href="https://twitter.com/TomRees_77">@TomRees_77</a> in which they neatly break down school leadership into six essential elements. All you have to do is attend to these and everything will be okay(!)</div>

<div>
<b>The 7 Persistent Problems of School Leadership:</b></div>
<div>
<ol>
<li><b>Purpose </b>- set direction, create shared purpose, enlist staff contribution</li>
<li><b>Culture </b>- a professional, supportive and development culture</li>
<li><b>Curriculum</b> - organise the curriculum and establish effective teaching</li>
<li><b>Behaviour</b> - attend to pupil behaviour and wider circumstances</li>
<li><b>Improvement </b>- analyse problems, plan and implement strategies for sustained improvement</li>
<li><b>Administration</b> - manage and efficient, effective and legally compliant organisation</li>
<li><b>Self </b>- develop personal expertise, self-efficacy and self-regulation</li>
</ol>
</div>
<div>
Pretty much everything you have to do as a school leader will fall into one of these categories. </div>

<div>
I'll be using this list to check that what I'm doing on a day-to-day basis is broadly balanced and doesn't neglect any one of these domains.</div>
