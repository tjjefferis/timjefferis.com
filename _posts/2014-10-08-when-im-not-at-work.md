Today's @teachthought 30-day blogging challenge asks bloggers:
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Do you have other hobbies/interests that you bring into your classroom teaching? Explain.</blockquote>
I'm very lucky. There is a big overlap between the things I enjoy and the things I must do in my day-to-day working life:<br/>

<ol>
<li>
I like tinkering with computers, finding new apps and widgets and putting these to use in the classroom. I just have to be careful that the cult of the new doesn't interfere with delivering the content I need to get across.
</li>
<li>
I spend most of my holidays up in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_District" shape="rect" target="_blank">Lake District</a>. This really is a geographers' paradise. I even weave in a weekend trip up to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borrowdale" shape="rect" target="_blank">Borrowdale</a> for an U6th module on glacial geomorphology. Seventh heaven.
</li>
<li>
I really enjoy reading and listening to podcasts. Current affairs interest me and so I am able to make my teaching relevant. Geographers - much maligned as pencil-sharpening rugger-buggers - actually have a lot to say about the world.

<!--more-->

</li>
<li>
I keep myself fit and recently have taken to setting myself big physical challenges. Last year I did <a href="http://www.cyclingforcharity.org/" shape="rect" target="_blank">this</a>, this year I intend to do <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bob_Graham_Round" shape="rect" target="_blank">this</a>. I like to think that these things give me a bit of a spring to my stride and make my lessons more energetic than they might otherwise be.
</li>
</ol>
