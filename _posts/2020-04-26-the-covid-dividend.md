<img alt="IN+ | Covid-19: measures and recommendations at IN+" src="https://encrypted-tbn0.gstatic.com/images?q=tbn%3AANd9GcQt_LnPqft4H2JdWhhMRTYukst5-m1YfeFHUNdWxTmy7Qx2n8GB&amp;usqp=CAU"/>When/if our current situation comes to an end I hope there will be some benefits that come out of it; not least in a bit of a change of priorities. Here are the things I hope I won't forget:<ol><li>Physical health is precious and is best maintained by regular physical exercise. In the past, I dropped exercise when things felt a bit too busy at work. In the future, I hope I'll drop the work when things feel a bit too busy to exercise.</li><li>Contact with friends and family makes you feel better and ultimately is what life is all about. These days it requires next to no effort. I hope I'll make more of an effort in the future to connect with the people who make me happy.</li><li>Always spend <i>way less</i> than you earn. Spending money doesn't make you happy, but not having to worry significantly reduces the chance that you'll be unhappy. Work towards being able to live in a crisis with at a burn rate that means you'll always be okay.</li><li>However annoying your current circumstances, there are people way worse off than you. Be grateful, be kind.</li></ol>
