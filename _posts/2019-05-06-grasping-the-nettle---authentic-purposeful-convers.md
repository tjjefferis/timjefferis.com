If you're a senior leader and you've not yet read Susan Scott's book '<a href="https://www.amazon.com/Fierce-Conversations-Achieving-Success-Conversation/dp/0425193373" target="_blank">Fierce Conversations</a>', I encourage you to do so. The book is a stirring call to action:<br/>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<i>Think about it. What are the conversations you've been unable or unwilling to have ... that if you were able to have would change everything?</i></blockquote>
She lays out some guidelines for dealing with the difficult people-problems that inevitably arise in our daily work. Don't chicken out, confront the issue and - in sixty seconds if possible - at the start of the meeting:<br/>
<br/>
<ol>
<li><i>Name the issue</i></li>
<li><i>Select a specific example of the behaviour or situation that you want to change</i></li>
<li><i>Describe your emotions about the issue</i></li>
<li><i>Clarify what is at stake</i></li>
<li><i>Identify your own contribution to the problem</i></li>
<li><i>Indicate your wish to resolve the issue</i></li>
<li><i>Invite your interlocutor to respond</i></li>
</ol>
<br/>
Other takeaways I got from the book were:<br/>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<i>For a leader, there is no trivial comment. Something you may not even remember saying may have a devastating impact o someone looking to you for guidance and approval.</i></blockquote>
When the need arises, be prepared to say something like:<br/>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<i>I am not happy with you right now.  In fact, I'm deeply angry and my intentions are less than noble, so how about having this conversation later.</i></blockquote>
Far better that than an explosion or, worse, tight-lipped silence.<br/>
<br/>
Instead of saying:<br/>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<i>You don't know what you're talking about.</i></blockquote>
Try saying:<br/>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<i>I have a different perspective.</i></blockquote>
Try to get yourself into a place in your professional and personal life where you can say, with conviction:<br/>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
<i>I am in the right place, with the people I love, doing the right work on purpose.</i></blockquote>
